## Project Overview

The project involved extensive use of CAD modeling, and 3D printing to prototype and finalize the design for a Howimat-inspired gear reducer. The Howimat design uses ball bearings driven by a cam to achieve zero-low backlash in a minimal profile. 

## Project Motivation

I am interested in eventually building my own CNC machine. One component of a good CNC machine is a gear reducer with low backlash. I found Dylan Edmiston's [video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bZBH35Yfn6k) where he used it in his head-head CNC machine. The Howimat design is also used in the highest end Heller CNC machines as seen [here](https://www.detlevhofmann.de/products/howimat-gear-type-units/?lang=en). I found it interesting how there have been plenty of attempts to make a harmonic drive or cycloidial drive but not a Howimat drive.

## Key Achievements

* CAD modelling of advanced shapes, such as the spline and cam
* Section analysis of final model to verify ball bearing movement
* 3D printing prototypes and final part
